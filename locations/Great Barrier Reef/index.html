The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system, located off the coast of Queensland, Australia. It spans over 2,300 kilometers (1,400 miles) and is made up of thousands of individual reefs and islands. Renowned for its extraordinary biodiversity and vibrant marine life, including colorful corals, fish, and other marine species, the Great Barrier Reef is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a globally significant natural wonder. It attracts millions of visitors each year for snorkeling, diving, and marine exploration, offering unforgettable experiences in one of the world's most diverse ecosystems.

The Grand Canyon is a massive geological formation located in Arizona, USA. Carved by the Colorado River over millions of years, it stretches for over 277 miles (446 kilometers) in length, reaches depths of over a mile (1.6 kilometers), and spans up to 18 miles (29 kilometers) in width. Renowned for its breathtaking vistas, colorful rock layers, and sheer size, the Grand Canyon is considered one of the natural wonders of the world. It attracts millions of visitors annually, offering opportunities for hiking, rafting, and sightseeing amidst its awe-inspiring landscapes.
